Fetal Growth Week by Week

Fetal growth follows a predictable curve. By 12 weeks, the baby is about 2 inches long. By 20 weeks (the halfway point), about 6.5 inches. At full term (40 weeks), the average baby is about 20 inches and 7.5 pounds.

These are 50th percentile values — half of babies are larger, half smaller. Your provider uses ultrasound to track your specific baby's growth curve.
